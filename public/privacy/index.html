Privacy

Last updated: July 3, 2026.

This page explains what smplmark collects, why, and what we do with it. We aim to collect as little as the Service needs to work.

What we collect

Account information. When you sign up we store your email address and basic account details. If you sign in with a third-party provider (such as Google or Microsoft), we receive the identity information that provider shares to authenticate you.

Content you publish. Benchmark data and related details you upload are stored so we can host and display them. Published benchmark data is public by design.

Technical data. Like most sites, our infrastructure processes standard request data (such as IP address and user agent) to operate the Service securely and reliably.

Cookies and sessions

We use a session cookie to keep you signed in. It is required for authenticated use of the Service. We do not use third-party advertising or tracking cookies.

How we use it

We use the data above to provide and secure the Service, authenticate you, host and serve published benchmarks, and communicate with you about your account. We do not sell your personal information.

Where it lives

smplmark runs on Cloudflare's platform, and your data is processed and stored on that infrastructure. We retain data for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Service and meet legal obligations.

Your choices

You can request access to or deletion of your account data by contacting us. Deleting your account removes your account information; published benchmark data may be removed on request.

Changes

We may update this page from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the "last updated" date above.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Email hello@smplmark.org.
