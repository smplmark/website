About smplmark

A home for benchmarks, built on a simple trust model.

smplmark is a general-purpose benchmark host. A publisher uploads benchmark data on a recurring basis; a visitor reviews benchmarks from the publishers they choose to trust. That is the whole model.

smplmark does not validate the truth of the data — only its shape. What the publisher publishes is what you get. Every benchmark declares the metrics it reports, and every number is fetchable as JSON or downloadable as CSV from the same public endpoint that powers the charts. If you trust the publisher, you can trust the numbers; if you don't, the raw data is right there to check.

Each benchmark is organized as targets (the things being measured), runs (a series of measurements from a target), and samples (the individual data points). Some values are stored exactly as the publisher sent them; others are derived — computed from the raw sample when it is read, so the stored data never commits to an interpretation and definitions can evolve without rewriting history.

Every published benchmark has an Overview, its Data, a Methodology writeup, and a Publisher — so you always know what you're looking at and who stands behind it. Browse what's published on the benchmarks page.

Data sources & attribution

Some benchmarks on smplmark republish openly licensed results from third-party sources. Each one names its source on the benchmark page and links back to the original data; the sources and their license terms are:

  • Blender Open Data — community Blender Benchmark results, dedicated to the public domain (CC0 1.0).
  • Stanford HELM — the HELM Capabilities leaderboard, publicly released by Stanford's Center for Research on Foundation Models as an open resource for the community. smplmark is not affiliated with or endorsed by Stanford University.
  • Hugging Face Open LLM Leaderboard — the retired leaderboard's final published dataset, released by Hugging Face as an open community resource.
  • OpenML — open machine-learning experiment results, licensed CC BY 4.0. Vanschoren et al., "OpenML: networked science in machine learning," SIGKDD Explorations 15(2), 2013.

Results are republished as facts, with attribution, and no source endorses smplmark. If you represent a source and want data corrected or removed, email support@smplmark.org — removals are fast.
